Transforming Raw Mineral Resources into Value-Added Products

Erfanian Mine Extraction and Processing is a strategic initiative aimed at establishing a state-of-the-art zinc processing facility in Afghanistan. With an initial investment ranging between $30 and $95 million, the project is planned in several phases—from early pilot operations processing 30,000–50,000 tons of zinc ore to full-scale production reaching 167,000 tons of processed ore and 50,000 tons of zinc ingots. This phased expansion is designed to boost Afghanistan’s share in the global mineral value chain while creating approximately 848 direct jobs and 1,696 indirect jobs.

Leveraging innovative technologies and sustainable practices, our approach integrates advanced processing techniques with rigorous quality control to meet international standards. A key element of our strategy is the invitation for strategic investment in Herat—capitalizing on its geographic advantages for exporting processed materials to neighboring markets such as Iran, Pakistan, and India. Our comprehensive plan marries financial discipline with operational excellence and a strong commitment to social and environmental responsibility.

Overview & Challenges

In today’s competitive global market, the demand for zinc is driven by sectors such as galvanizing, alloy production, and battery manufacturing. Our project faces several challenges including global price volatility, ensuring consistent ore quality, and managing a complex supply chain. We mitigate these risks through long-term supply agreements, continuous research and development to optimize processing, and diversified market strategies aimed at both domestic and international clients.
